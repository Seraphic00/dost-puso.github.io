HISTORY

The DOST-PSU UCC Scholars’ Organization, affectionately known as DOST-PUSO, had its humble beginnings on October 1, 2022. Founded by a group of passionate Department of Science and Technology (DOST) scholars at Pangasinan State University's Urdaneta City Campus, the organization's inception marked the commencement of a remarkable journey.

As DOST-PUSO continues to grow, adapt, and evolve, it remains steadfast in its mission to inspire and empower scholars, promote scientific literacy, and serve the nation with unwavering dedication. The organization's journey is a testament to what can be achieved when passionate individuals come together with a shared vision, driven by a love for education, science, and service to the Philippines.

In a short span of time, DOST-PUSO achieved remarkable milestones. The organization became a hub for fostering academic excellence, nurturing leadership skills, and promoting patriotic values. It organized numerous training workshops, seminars, and outreach programs that not only enhanced the skills and knowledge of its members but also extended the benefits of science and technology to the wider community.

DOST PUSO LOGO

The official logo of the DOST-PSU UCC Scholars’ Organization is its official symbol. Each element represents the following:

(a) The three-color scheme of the logo was inspired by the DOST Logo and represents the unknown (black), truth and enlightenment (white), and progress (blue)
(b)The heart-shaped logo was inspired by the organization's name, "PUSO," which means "heart.
(c) The chain-like structure of the logo represents the shared bonds among the patriot scholars of PSU UCC, which are strengthened by the organization's mission and vision.
(d) The square in the center of the logo, which was formed by the interlocking of the two chains represents each side the organization's four core values: Patriotic, Unwavering Service, Servant Leadership, and Optimistic.

Vision

The organization shall envision a community of scholars who are Passionate about serving the nation, Unified by the same aspiration, Steadfast in the face of adversity, and Optimistic toward creating a better country.

Mission

The DOST-PSU UCC Scholars' Organization (DOST-PUSO) shall strive to pursue:
Promote development of the nation's most steadfast, enthusiastic, innovative, and patriotic scholars through relevant trainings and workshops, collaboration, leadership and academic excellence;
Unify DOST scholars of PSU UCC with one vision and mission;
Support, empower, and encourage patriot scholars to take an active role in nation building; and
Offer unwavering act of service to the nation.

The Department of Science and Technology had once again organized a leadership camp for the 150 DOST-SEI scholars of Region 1 and CAR from March 28 to April 1, 2023, at Thunderbird Resort, San Fernando City, La Union. Bringing a total of nine (9) participants from the DOST-PSU Urdaneta Scholars' Organization (DOST-PUSO), the scholars enthusiastically engaged in the whole period of the camp, which seeks to mold the scholars' leadership and core values. Junior Level Science Scholarship

Types of Scholarship
MERIT. The DOST-SEI Merit Scholarship Program, formerly known as the NSDB or NSTA Scholarship under RA No. 2067, is awarded to students with high aptitude in science and mathematics and are willing to pursue careers in the fields of science and technology.
RA 7687. Republic Act No. 7687, also known as the “Science and Technology Scholarship Act of 1994”, provides for scholarships to talented and deserving students whose families’ socio-economic status does not exceed the set cut-off values of certain indicators. Qualifiers must pursue priority fields of study in the basic sciences, engineering, other applied sciences, and science and mathematics teaching.
